Chapter 1: Knowing Our Numbers

Numbers are everywhere around us. We use numbers to count things, measure distances, tell time, and much more. In this chapter, we will learn about different types of numbers and how to work with them.

1.1 Comparing Numbers

When we have two numbers, we can compare them to find out which one is larger or smaller. For example:
- 45 is greater than 32
- 128 is less than 256
- 500 is equal to 500

We use symbols to show comparison:
- > means "greater than"
- < means "less than"  
- = means "equal to"

Example: 75 > 50 (75 is greater than 50)

1.2 Large Numbers

As we count higher, numbers become very large. We use place value to understand large numbers:
- Ones place
- Tens place
- Hundreds place
- Thousands place
- Ten thousands place
- Lakhs place

Example: In the number 45,678:
- 8 is in the ones place
- 7 is in the tens place
- 6 is in the hundreds place
- 5 is in the thousands place
- 4 is in the ten thousands place
